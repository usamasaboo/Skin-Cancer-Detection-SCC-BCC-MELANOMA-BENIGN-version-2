Melanoma

Most serious skin cancer developing in melanocytes. Key: Multiple colors & irregular shape.

ABCDE Rule: Asymmetry, Border (irregular), Color (variant), Diameter (>6mm), Evolving.
Appearance: Dark brown, black, or multi-colored; asymmetrical with irregular borders.
Texture: Flat or slightly raised; can look like a suspicious mole.
High Risk
Basal Cell (BCC)

Common slow-growing cancer. Key: Shiny/Pearly bump with smooth edges.

Appearance: Pearly/shiny bump, pink or skin-colored.
Vessels: Telangiectasia (thin visible blood vessels) often present.
Common Areas: Nose, face, neck. Smooth and shiny texture.
Moderate Risk
Squamous Cell (SCC)

Can spread if untreated. Key: Scaly / rough texture with crusty surface.

Appearance: Rough, scaly, often red/pink; may look like a crusty sore.
Shape: Irregular, thick, raised lesion; dry and flaky texture.
Common Areas: Face, ears, hands (sun-exposed skin).
Moderate Risk
Benign Indicators

Common markers of a low-risk, non-malignant lesion.

Symmetrical → Both halves of the lesion look almost identical.
Round Edges → Borders are smooth, sharp, and well-defined.
Single Color → Uniform brown or tan; no sudden color shifts.
Stable → No evolution in size, shape, or color over time.
Low Risk
